## Version 1: Technical Focus (For Tech Community)

🚀 **MetalNLLB: Bringing AI Translation to $10 Devices**

Last year, we announced Tushe - The Foundry, building foundational datasets and models for Hausa and African languages.

Today, we're solving an even more fundamental problem: **infrastructure**.

**The Reality:**
Africa has a 19-year-old median age. Half our population is at school age. But they lack:
- Reliable internet access
- Affordable devices
- Consistent electricity
- Access to quality STEM education in their mother tongue

**The Solution: MetalNLLB**

We've built a bare-metal neural machine translation engine that:
✅ Runs on $10 ARM boards (Raspberry Pi Zero)
✅ Works 100% offline - no internet needed
✅ Uses only 30MB RAM - 80% less than standard frameworks
✅ Translates 200+ languages with 100% quality parity
✅ 3.47x faster than industry-standard CTranslate2
✅ Pure C implementation - 2,700 lines, zero dependencies

**Why This Matters:**

Imagine:
- A farmer in rural Nigeria getting crop advice in Hausa, offline
- A doctor in a remote clinic diagnosing patients with AI assistance in Yoruba
- Students in village schools learning physics and engineering in their mother tongue
- Teachers delivering world-class STEM education without internet

**The Vision:**

We're building Kindle-like devices with AI baked into silicon. No cloud. No internet. Just intelligence in your pocket, responding in your language.

As Jeffrey Sachs said: "This has to be the whole continent in learning right now... Make sure they're learning and then you get the transformation beyond anything we can dream today."

**China became the "country of engineers" by educating their youth in STEM.**
**Africa can become the next continent of engineers by 2045.**
